Welcome to MassData, the new Commonwealth open data website. We believe that the public good is served through transparency and open access. By being responsible custodians of open public data in Massachusetts, we can meet the ideal of data by the people, for the people.

Keep up with the latest data news at the Commonwealth by following @MassData on Twitter.

About open data and the MassData initiative

MassData is intended to be a "one-stop shop" that provides easy access to already available, open data. MassData highlights the Commonwealth's commitment to transparency in government. Through the open data portal, we centralize data from a number of state agencies, as well as municipal data from three cities: Boston, Somerville, and Cambridge.

PUTTING TECHNOLOGY TO WORK

The MassData initiative serves to increase public awareness of and access to government data, enhance government transparency and accountability, and encourage economic development and civic engagement.

UNLOCKING OPPORTUNITY

MassData is a place for research, visualization and the exploration of existing public data sets in the Commonwealth. Providing easy, user-friendly access to open data is a conerstone of the Commonwealth's digital strategy for driving innovation.

By consolidating and publishing open state and local data, government can help technologists, businesses and residents develop new solutions that support and catalyze the work of state government.

OPEN DATA & PRIVACY

By definition, open data is data that is not considered private, secure, or confidential, and that is not protected by law.

The Commonwealth protects personal, confidential and other private data, ensuring that such protected data not be released in violation of applicable legal constraints or the public interest.

FEDERATED CITIES

The Federated Cities Partnership is a first-of-its-kind collaboration with the state and the three cities of Boston, Cambridge, and Somerville.

This working partnership signals a new era of collaboration between state and municipal government. It does not replace any state or municipal open data efforts; rather it supports and builds upon open data use across political boundaries.
