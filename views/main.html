At the Commonwealth of Massachusetts, we believe that the public good is served through open access and transparency. As responsible custodians of open public data in Massachusetts, we're working to meet the ideal of data by the people, for the people.

About the Commonwealth's open data initiative

MassData highlights the Commonwealth's commitment to transparency in government.

This site is intended to be a one-stop shop that provides easy access to existing and new sources of open data. We aggregate data from a number of state agencies and municipal data from several participating communities.

PUTTING TECHNOLOGY TO WORK

Through MassData, the Commonwealth aims to increase public awareness of and access to government data, enhance government transparency and accountability, and encourage economic development and civic engagement.

UNLOCKING OPPORTUNITY

MassData is a place for research, visualization and the exploration of existing public data sets in the Commonwealth. Providing easy, user-friendly access to open data is a conerstone of the Commonwealth's digital strategy for driving innovation.

By consolidating and publishing open state and local data, government can help technologists, businesses and residents develop new solutions that support and catalyze the work of state government.

OPEN DATA & PRIVACY

The Commonwealth continually strives to protect personal, confidential, and other private data.

By definition, open data is data that is not considered private, secure, or confidential, and that is not protected by law.

NEW MUNICIPAL ENGAGEMENT

The Commonwealth is developing new collaborative partnerships with municipalities in the state. We believe it signals the start of a new era of cross-boundary partnership focused on driving government transparency.

This effort does not replace any state or municipal open data efforts; rather it supports and builds upon open data use across political boundaries.
